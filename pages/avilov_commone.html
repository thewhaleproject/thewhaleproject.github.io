Sviatoslav Avilov and Davide Commone

Sounddesign for Maria Li's installation in Venice, Italy

A collaboration Within The Whale Project brings the soundscapes Underwater of Sviatoslav Avilov and Suite from Manifold Voyager of Davide Commone to accompany the exhibition of Maria Li in Castello, Venice.

Underwater is a sound track inspired by the eutrophication, a phenomenon also called the "blooming" of waters in many lagoons around the world, a cyclical and progressive process which have serious repercussions for natural systems, leading to their self-destruction, brought about by excessive prosperity.

Sounds recorded in the marshed of Venetian lagoon in the zones of excess autrophication were used as inspiration for the creation of the electronic background and melodic lines. The track is a process of submerging into the deep and witnessing the extent to which artificial tecnology influences underwater life. It's a reflection on a society of abundance, how this society reverberates as repetitive and noise and lacks direction.




The Manifold Voyager Suite is an exploration of a corpora containing synthetic sounds genereated by no input mixing and prerecorded sounds of Venetian landscapes.

It's a result of research of how the categories of form and structure as understood in musical composition. A part of the production was dine by an algorithm that implements recursive rules on discrete corpora of sounds, treating each corpus as a set of symbols from which to generate new parallel versions of the same corpus.
